The Keys Today!

The opening never happened! On December 2016 the developer who was massivley over on budget and timescale once again approached the bank for more of both. Portugese state owned bank Caixa Geral de Depósitos who were the principal backer refused.

The proposed rescue plan was rejected and the bank put the project into liquidation while itself being owed almost €279 million, and finally pulled the plug on the project on January 2017.

The developer valued the finished project at almost €400 million however this does seem to be somewhat optimistic.

The original loans had been sanctioned by the then loans boss for Caixa Geral, ex corrupt politcian and convicted fraudster Armando Vara, (The man who was suspected of taking bribes for loans) this could possibly explain the banks lack of due diligance on this project.

On overall inspection the site would appear to be about 90% complete, however it now lies derelict and is falling into disrepair. The lagoon/river surrounding the central apartments is delapidated and empty with only rain water pools, dirt and debris as features.

The walkways and bridges are disintegrating with trees and planting unkept and overgrown. The buildings look to be mainly complete and in most cases watertight with most having windows and doors fitted. The interiors however are bare brick and concrete with no fixtures or fittings to speak of.

Strangley some are finished in a way that looks almost dressed, like a facade to possibly appear further on than perhaps they were, maybe for some publicity materials or prospective clients to view.
